## The Emerging Threat of Synthetic Identity Fraud
Synthetic identity fraud is a growing concern, with losses soaring to **$35 billion** in 2023, according to FiVerity. This type of fraud involves combining real and fake data to create new identities, which can then be used to open accounts, take loans, and disappear with your money. The Federal Reserve notes that GenAI is a 'volatile accelerant' in this domain, making it a systemic vulnerability. To combat this threat, banks are investing in AI-powered solutions that can detect and prevent synthetic identity fraud. For example, **machine learning algorithms** can be used to analyze transaction data and identify patterns that are indicative of synthetic identity fraud.
