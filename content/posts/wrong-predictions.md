**When Predictions Go Wrong: How Mistakes Can Lead to Innovation**

Napoleon's disastrous invasion of Russia in 1812 is a prime example of how **overconfidence** can lead to **catastrophic consequences**. This **historical blunder** serves as a reminder that even the most **brilliant minds** can fall victim to **hubris**. To avoid making similar mistakes, it's essential to **stay humble** and **open-minded**, considering multiple perspectives and **evaluating evidence** before making a decision. Thomas Edison's **initial rejection** of alternating current (AC) in favor of direct current (DC) is another notable example of a **wrong prediction**. Today, AC powers the world, and Edison's mistake serves as a reminder of the importance of **embracing new ideas** and **being open to change**.
